#### Engine 1: The Private Mortgage Capital Cycle

**Section Header:** Engine 1 — Capital velocity, not credit risk

**Copy:**

A traditional MIC originates a mortgage, holds it for 12-24 months, and collects interest. The return is bounded by the interest rate on the loan minus operating costs. If the underwriting is conservative, the interest rate is moderate. Conservative underwriting and above-market returns are structurally incompatible in an originate-and-hold model.

FairLend's capital cycle breaks that constraint.

**Step 1: Originate**
Investor capital is deployed into a vetted private mortgage. FairLend earns a 1% lending fee on origination. Every deal has passed a 90% rejection filter, double appraisals, conservative LTV limits, and multi-layered AI and human underwriting — the same standards any disciplined MIC would apply.

**Step 2: Hold (Maximum 60 Days)**
The mortgage remains on the books for a maximum of 2 months — not 12-24 months. Short duration means less exposure to market shifts, rate changes, and borrower deterioration. This is a risk reduction, not a risk increase.

**Step 3: Distribute**
Through institutional relationships and distribution channels built over nearly 30 years, FairLend sells the mortgage to qualified buyers. These channels didn't appear overnight — they are the product of decades of relationship building that a newer manager cannot replicate on day one. Investor capital is freed.

**Step 4: Redeploy**
That capital cycles back into the next vetted mortgage, earning another origination fee. The same dollar of invested capital generates multiple fee-earning cycles per year.

**The math:** A traditional MIC earns one return stream per dollar deployed. FairLend earns multiple origination fees per dollar per year, on mortgages underwritten to the same conservative standards. The outperformance comes from capital velocity — not from looser underwriting, higher LTVs, or riskier borrowers.
